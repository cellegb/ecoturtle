Types of Pollution

Air

Not only is air pollution bad for health, but it is also dangerous to the environment. One of the biggest air pollutants is caused from motor vehicle. Motor vehicles release global warming and greenhouse pollutants which cause those events. The emissions given off by motor vehicle pollution can lead to various health problems such as asthma, heart disease, chronic bronchitis, cancer, and premature death Because of these dangerous side effects of motor vehicle pollution, the issue needs to be solved.

Water

Water pollution is the contamination of water bodies such as rivers, lakes, oceans and groundwaters that are a result of human activity. One of the biggest water pollutants is plastic which has been accumulating in the lakes and oceans and can endure thousands of years while it travels from coast to coast harming marine life. Oceanographers say water pollution is not only having an impact on our idyllic paradises but our ecosystem as a whole.

Plastic

Plastic has now been around for over a century and now is used in almost every industry and application, but plastic no longer has a positive reputation in the United States. In the 1960’s plastic debris was first observed in the ocean which resulted in an increase in American awareness for environmental issues. The dangers of chemical pesticides on the environment have been exposed as more studies have been tested alongside concerns about the increasing of plastic pollution. During the 1970’s and 1980’s, the reputation of plastic fell further down as anxiety about waste expanded. Plastic pollution has become an epidemic in the United States that will only worsen if simple changes aren’t made.
